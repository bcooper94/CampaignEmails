New Hampshire Voter Email Corpus 2016

  • Machine Learning 1: For the first machine learning task we looked at emails from Clinton and Trump and attempted to determine if they came before or after the party conventions. So in other words did their emails change after they became the candidate for their party. The features we extracted were unigrams of the emails. We looked at nouns, verbs, and adjectives of length longer than three to attempt to get rid of filler words. Our results were around 40% prediction which wasn’t great as you would think a flip of a coin would be 50% but there were less after emails than before so just guessing wasn’t 50 50, so this results wasn’t terrible. A side processing step we did was compare a frequency distribution of the two candidates emails before and after. An interesting feature we found in the frequency distribution is that clinton asked for money a lot before but rarely afterwards.
